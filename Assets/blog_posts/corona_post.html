Things you can do to protect yourself
from the corona virus

Connect with me:

        
By Natnael Bekabtu on March 10, 2020
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Since the first case was reported in the Wuhan province of china in december, the number of cases
of people with the corona virus has increased rapidly. The origin of the virus is traced to an animal
market in Wuhan. Even though the virus has killed a little over 4500 people world wide, the total number
of reported cases so far has reached 120,000 making the mortality rate of about 2%. This implies that the
virus is far less deadly than SARS (an outbreak recorded in 2003 with mortality rate of 10%) or MERS
(asimilar outbreak that reached a mortality rate of 34%) but is far mor transmittable. The WHO has
declared the disease as a pandemic today with president Tewodros Admassu outlining some precautions to
take to avoid contracting the virus has outline some precautions. Even though there is no known cure or
vaccine for the illness, here are some of the steps you can take to keep yourself healthy.

  1. Don't Panic: Even though the virus is infectious it is far less deadly than previous outbreaks like
    the Ebola virus. This means that even if you have the virus there is a high probability of survival.
  2. Avoid wearing surgical masks: surgical masks are designed for doctors so that they can avoid splatters
    during surgery from patients; It is not a proven scientific fact that they can prevent transmission of the virus
    A better alternative is a N95 or N99 fitted masks which have built in air filterers.
  3. Keep persona hygiene: the virus is mainly transmitted through cough droplets (much like the common cold) so
    having good hygiene like washing hands repeatedly, avoid touching surfaces can go a long way in stopping
    the transmission cycle
  4. Avoid touching face: Make sure you wash your hands after you've touched any surface.
  5. Avoid crowded places: Crowded places have high probability of infection from the virus.
    so as much as possible people should avoid them.
  6. Self-quarantine: If you have been showing symptoms of the illness or has recently been to
    a country with known cases avoid contact with people and seek the advice of health professionals.

It is important to find the balance between hysteria and negligence. But it is also wise to remember that
if you follow the necessary there is no reason to panic.
